## 1. Foundational Premise

### 1.1 Deterministic Occlusion Model

There are **no probabilistic orbitals** and **no "penetration"** into a nucleus. Each electron exists as a real displacement vortex tethered to its paired proton through an internal spation tension vector. As the nucleus rotates, these tethers oscillate and trace fixed geometric cycles determined by the nuclear frame topology.

**Key principle:** The apparent "shells" are rings of equilibrium in the pressure lattice, not energy wells; their ordering follows a strict sequence of geometric compaction.

### 1.2 Core Mechanism: Deterministic Occlusion

Every electron excludes spations, forming a pressure shadow cone along lines connecting it to the nucleus. The nucleus itself rotates above the lattice critical pressure limit, creating alternating high- and low-pressure poles. Each additional electron-proton pair is locked into the next stable direction set that maintains balance in total occlusion.

**Occlusion fraction** for direction $\hat{\Omega}_k$:
$$E(\hat{\Omega}_k) = \frac{1}{4\pi} \sum_{j=1}^{N_{\text{core}}} \chi_j(\hat{\Omega}_k) \Omega_{j \to \hat{\Omega}_k} \tag{1.1}$$

where $\chi_j = 1$ if the inner site eclipses the nucleus along $\hat{\Omega}_k$ and $0$ otherwise. 

**Critical point:** No averaging over continuous solid angles occurs; only the finite directions available on the dodecardinal frame are counted.

## 3. Binding and Energy from Occlusion Balance

### 3.1 Effective Pressure

The binding pressure at an electron site is the unoccluded nuclear pressure:
$$P_{\text{eff}}(\hat{\Omega}_k) = P_0 \left(\frac{R_N}{r_{n\ell}}\right)^3 \left[1 - E(\hat{\Omega}_k)\right] \tag{3.1}$$

where:
- $P_0$ is the unperturbed nuclear pressure
- $R_N$ is the nuclear radius
- $r_{n\ell}$ is the radial distance for quantum numbers $(n,\ell)$
- $E(\hat{\Omega}_k)$ is the occlusion fraction from Eq. (1.1)

### 3.2 Averaged Occlusion Factor

Averaging over all waypoints of the site-cycle $\Gamma_{n\ell}$ gives:
$$\Xi_{n\ell} = 1 - \frac{1}{M_{n\ell}} \sum_{k=1}^{M_{n\ell}} E(\hat{\Omega}_k) \tag{3.2}$$

where $M_{n\ell}$ is the number of discrete directions in the cycle.

### 3.3 Energy Formula

The energy relative to hydrogenic binding is:
$$\boxed{E_{n\ell} = E_H \frac{Z^2 \Xi_{n\ell}}{n^2}} \tag{3.3}$$

where $E_H$ is the hydrogen ground state energy.

**Key insight:** $\Xi_{n\ell}$ replaces any screening constant; the factor is **purely geometric**.

## 4. Quantum Defect Correspondence (Without Quantum)

### 4.1 Quantum Defect Definition

Experimental "quantum defects" $\delta_\ell$ simply record geometric occlusion. The standard quantum defect formula is:
$$E_{n\ell} = -\frac{R_\infty hc Z^2}{(n - \delta_\ell)^2}$$

### 4.2 SDT Derivation

Equating with SDT formula (Eq. 3.3):
$$\frac{1}{(n - \delta_\ell)^2} = \frac{Z^2 \Xi_{n\ell}}{n^2}$$

Solving for $\delta_\ell$:
$$\boxed{\delta_\ell = n \left(1 - \sqrt{\frac{\Xi_{n\ell}}{Z^2}}\right) = n \left(1 - \frac{\sqrt{\Xi_{n\ell}}}{Z}\right)} \tag{4.1}$$

For small corrections, this simplifies to:
$$\delta_\ell \approx n \left(1 - \frac{\Xi_{n\ell}}{Z}\right) \tag{4.2}$$

**Physical meaning:** No probabilities and no fields are invoked - only the proportion of nuclear pressure preserved along the allowed directions of that cycle.

## 5. Why $\delta_s > \delta_p > \delta_d$

### 5.1 Geometric Explanation

The ordering arises strictly from stack sequence, not from penetration:

- **$s$ cycles** use polar directions: least occluded, strongest pressure, largest $\delta$
- **$p$ cycles** lie on the equatorial offset ring: moderate occlusion, mid $\delta$
- **$d$ cycles** occupy directions already eclipsed by cube and ring sets: maximum occlusion, $\delta \approx 0$

### 5.2 Occlusion Factors

Typical values:
- $\Xi_s \approx 0.6-0.8$ → $\delta_s \approx 1-2$
- $\Xi_p \approx 0.8-0.9$ → $\delta_p \approx 0.5-1$
- $\Xi_d \approx 0.95-0.99$ → $\delta_d \approx 0-0.1$

This ordering is **universal** and follows directly from the geometric structure.

## 7. Scaling to Heavier Atoms

### 7.1 Z-Dependence

As $Z$ increases, nuclear compaction raises rotational velocity and narrows the angular gaps. The same geometry then yields larger $\delta$ values roughly scaling with $(Z - 1)/n$, matching empirical sequences (Na → K → Rb) without invoking electron clouds.

**Scaling law:**
$$\delta_\ell(Z) \approx \delta_\ell(1) \times \frac{Z - 1}{n} \tag{7.1}$$
